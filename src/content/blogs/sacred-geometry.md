title: "Observations on Sacred Geometry & Mathematics"
date: "October 14th, 1542"
---
The ancient philosophers believed that the universe is governed by mathematical ratios.

Here is an example of the Golden Ratio ($\phi$):

$$
\phi = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2} \approx 1.6180339887
$$

This equation represents the fundamental essence of geometric progression.

![Golden Ratio](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Golden_ratio_line.svg/800px-Golden_ratio_line.svg.png)

When we observe nature, from the arrangement of leaves to the spiral of galaxies, the math remains invariant. Notice how integrating over certain continuous functions reveals beauty:

$$
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} dx = \sqrt{\pi}
$$

Such forms of knowledge were carefully curated in grand illuminated manuscripts.
